Master Of Cermonies

William Pate

President and CEO of the Atlanta convention and visitors Bureau

A long time friend of AMA Atlanta and a leader in the Atlanta marketing community, we are honored to have William Pate, once again, as the 2016 Master of Ceremonies. As president of the Atlanta Convention and Visitors Bureau, William Pate is in charge of maintaining tourism as one of the cities top economic drivers. The industry brought more than 45 million visitors to metro Atlanta in 2013, generating more than $13 billion in visitor spending in sustaining more than 23,000 jobs.

Prior to joining ACVB, Pate served as president of Career Sports and Entertainment, a national sports marketing and representation firm. He is also the former chief marketing officer of BellSouth, one of the worldâ€™s largest communications companies, where he spearheaded the development and marketing of the BellSouth brand both nationally and internationally.

Pate also held positions at MCI, where he supervised domestic and international advertising and public relations, and at Knapp Inc., an Atlanta-based marketing services firm. He began his career in non-profit, working in public relations positions with both Goodwill industries and the American Red Cross.

Pate has received many awards and honors for his work from both advertising Age and the American marketing Association. The Atlanta Business Chronicle has named Pate to its list of the 100 Most Influential Atlantans for the past five years running, and in 2014, he was voted one of the Atlanta Business Chronicle's most admired CEO's. In 2010, Pate was honored as The Corporate Marketer of the year by the Atlanta chapter of the American marketing Association. In 2011, the Hospitality Sales & Marketing Association recognized Pate as one of the Top 25 Most Extraordinary Minds in Marketing and Sales.

He serves on several boards, including Children's Healthcare of Atlanta, the College Football Hall of Fame, the Commerce Club, the Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce and the Chick-fil-A Peach Bowl. Pate also serves on the Board of Counselors of the Carter Presidential Center and the Industry Advisory Board for Georgia State University's Cecil B. Day School of Hospitality Administration. Most recently, Pate was named to the Woodruff Arts Center's Board of Trustees. Pate is a past chairman of the board of the Atlanta sports Council, the Chic-fil-A Peach Bowl and the ACVB

A native of Atlanta, Pate grew up in Decatur and attended Georgia State University where he received his undergraduate degree in journalism and his graduate degree in communications.
